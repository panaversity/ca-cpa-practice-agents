# Pakistan Income Tax Ordinance 2001 Reference

## Structure of the Ordinance

The ITO 2001 is organised into 13 chapters and 7 schedules:

- Chapter I: Preliminary (definitions)
- Chapter II: Charge to Tax (sections 4-8)
- Chapter III: Tax on Taxable Income (sections 9-14)
- Chapter IV: Computation of Income (sections 15-58)
- Chapter V: Exemptions and Tax Concessions (sections 59-65)
- Chapter VI: Tax Credits (sections 65A-65H)
- Chapter VII: Collection and Recovery (sections 148-165)
- Chapter X: Penalties and Offences (sections 181-191)

## Schedules

- **First Schedule**: Tax rates (Part I: individuals/AOP, Part II: companies)
- **Second Schedule**: Exemptions and tax concessions (Parts I-IV)
- **Third Schedule**: Depreciation rates by asset class
- **Seventh Schedule**: Banking company provisions

## Common Add-Backs (Section 21)

When preparing tax computations, these are the most frequently encountered disallowed deductions:

1. Any provision for bad debts (unless actually written off per s.29)
2. Capital expenditure treated as revenue in accounts
3. Personal expenses of partners/members
4. Payments without prescribed documentation (>PKR 50,000 cash)
5. Excessive perquisites beyond prescribed limits
6. Fines and penalties paid to government

## FBR E-Filing Portal

Tax returns are filed electronically through the FBR IRIS portal (https://iris.fbr.gov.pk). The portal requires:

- National Tax Number (NTN) or CNIC registration
- Digital signature (for companies)
- XML upload capability for large returns
