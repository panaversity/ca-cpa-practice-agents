## Financial Statement Components (IAS 1)

A complete set of financial statements comprises:

1. Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet)
2. Statement of Profit or Loss and Other Comprehensive Income
3. Statement of Changes in Equity
4. Statement of Cash Flows
5. Notes (significant accounting policies and explanatory information)

## Current vs Non-Current Classification (IAS 1.66-76)

An asset is current when it is:

- Expected to be realised within 12 months or the normal operating cycle
- Held primarily for trading
- Cash or cash equivalent (unless restricted for >12 months)

A liability is current when it is:

- Expected to be settled within 12 months or the normal operating cycle
- Held primarily for trading
- The entity does not have an unconditional right to defer settlement for >12 months

## Disclosure Drafting Checklist

For each material balance, verify the following disclosures are drafted:

- Accounting policy applied
- Breakdown of the balance by category
- Movement schedule (for non-current items)
- Judgment and estimation uncertainty (IAS 1.125)
- Related party components (IAS 24)
- Fair value information where required (IFRS 13)
