# Materiality and Sampling Reference

## Materiality Calculation Worksheet

### Step-by-Step Calculation

1. **Identify the benchmark**: Select from pre-tax profit, revenue, or total assets based on entity characteristics.

2. **Determine the appropriate percentage**:
   - Pre-tax profit: 3-5% (lower for public interest entities)
   - Revenue: 0.5-1% (lower for thin-margin businesses)
   - Total assets: 1-2% (for asset-intensive entities)

3. **Calculate overall materiality**: Benchmark amount x percentage = overall materiality.

4. **Calculate performance materiality**: Overall materiality x 50-75% (lower percentage for higher-risk engagements).

5. **Calculate trivial threshold**: Overall materiality x 5%.

### Benchmark Selection Decision Tree

- Is the entity profit-making with stable profits? -> Use pre-tax profit
- Is the entity loss-making? -> Use revenue
- Is the entity asset-intensive with volatile earnings? -> Use total assets
- Is the entity a not-for-profit? -> Use total expenses
- Is the entity newly established? -> Use revenue (no profit history)

### Worked Example

Entity: Manufacturing company, PKR 500M revenue, PKR 40M pre-tax profit (stable)

- Benchmark: Pre-tax profit (PKR 40M)
- Percentage: 5% (standard commercial entity)
- Overall materiality: PKR 2.0M
- Performance materiality: PKR 1.3M (65% — standard risk)
- Trivial threshold: PKR 100K (5%)

## Sampling Reference Tables

### Substantive Testing Sample Sizes

These are minimum sample sizes. Increase for higher assessed risk or when prior year exceptions were found.

| Population  | Low Risk | Medium Risk | High Risk |
| ----------- | -------- | ----------- | --------- |
| < 50        | All      | All         | All       |
| 50-250      | 10       | 15          | 25        |
| 251-500     | 15       | 25          | 40        |
| 501-1,000   | 20       | 30          | 50        |
| 1,001-5,000 | 25       | 40          | 60        |
| > 5,000     | 30       | 45          | 70        |

### Controls Testing Sample Sizes

| Control Frequency    | Sample Size | Basis                           |
| -------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------- |
| Multiple times daily | 25          | One per working day for a month |
| Daily                | 25          | One per working day for a month |
| Weekly               | 5           | Covers >10% of occurrences      |
| Monthly              | 2           | Covers ~17% of occurrences      |
| Quarterly            | 4           | All occurrences                 |
| Semi-annual          | 2           | All occurrences                 |
| Annual               | 1           | The single occurrence           |

### Exception Handling Rules

- Zero exceptions: Conclude the control is operating effectively (or the balance is fairly stated).
- One exception: Extend sample by 50%. If no further exceptions, conclude with a noted deviation. If more exceptions found, treat as two-or-more.
- Two or more exceptions: The control cannot be relied upon (or the balance may be materially misstated). Redesign audit approach — move to substantive testing (for controls) or expand procedures (for balances).
