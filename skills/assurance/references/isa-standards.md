## Audit Opinion Decision Tree

1. Are there material misstatements? If NO -> Unmodified opinion
2. If YES: Is the misstatement pervasive? If NO -> Qualified opinion. If YES -> Adverse opinion.
3. Is there a scope limitation? If YES and not pervasive -> Qualified opinion. If YES and pervasive -> Disclaimer of opinion.

## Risk Assessment Framework (ISA 315)

Risk of material misstatement = Inherent risk x Control risk

Inherent risk factors (ISA 315.A6):

- Complexity of transactions
- Subjectivity in measurement
- Susceptibility to misstatement due to management bias
- Change in business or accounting standards

Control risk assessment requires understanding of:

- Control environment
- Entity's risk assessment process
- Information system and communication
- Control activities
- Monitoring of controls

## Evidence Hierarchy (ISA 500)

From most to least reliable:

1. External evidence obtained directly by the auditor (bank confirmation)
2. External evidence obtained through the entity (supplier statement)
3. Internal evidence with strong controls (system-generated reports with audit trail)
4. Internal evidence with weak controls (manual records)
5. Oral representations from management (least reliable, must be corroborated)
