# Client Profile Guide

## How to Build a Client Entity Extension

This guide walks through the process of populating the client-entity SKILL.md template for a specific client. A well-built client profile is the single highest-impact customisation in the CA/CPA agent stack — it transforms generic agent outputs into client-relevant analysis.

## Information Gathering Checklist

### Entity Overview (gather from engagement files)

- [ ] Legal name and registration details
- [ ] Entity type (public, private, AOP, individual)
- [ ] Industry classification and principal activities
- [ ] Latest financial summary (revenue, total assets, employees)
- [ ] Fiscal year end and reporting framework
- [ ] Key management and governance structure

### Revenue and Seasonality (gather from management accounts)

- [ ] Revenue streams with approximate percentage breakdown
- [ ] Seasonal patterns (which quarters are high/low and why)
- [ ] Working capital cycle and how it relates to revenue patterns
- [ ] Effect of seasonality on key financial ratios

### Related Parties (gather from IAS 24 disclosures and engagement records)

- [ ] Shareholder structure and control relationships
- [ ] Related party transactions (nature, annual value, pricing basis)
- [ ] Key management personnel compensation arrangements
- [ ] Accounting and disclosure implications

### Risk Areas (gather from audit files, management discussions, prior year findings)

- [ ] Top 5 financial risks specific to this entity
- [ ] For each risk: what could go wrong, what is the financial exposure, what controls are in place
- [ ] Prior year audit findings that remain relevant
- [ ] Known regulatory compliance risks

### Management Preferences (gather from engagement experience)

- [ ] CFO's preferred reporting format and level of detail
- [ ] Reporting frequency and deadlines
- [ ] Key KPIs the management team monitors
- [ ] Communication preferences (email, board pack format, meeting style)

### Engagement History (gather from prior year files)

- [ ] Prior year audit opinion and any modifications
- [ ] Key audit matters from prior year
- [ ] Management letter findings (resolved and carried forward)
- [ ] Prior year tax positions and any ongoing disputes

## Quality Criteria for a Complete Profile

A client entity extension is production-ready when:

1. Every section has entity-specific content (no template placeholders remaining)
2. Seasonal patterns are quantified (not just "revenue is higher in Q4" but "Q4 represents 40% of annual revenue")
3. Risk areas include financial exposure estimates
4. Related party transactions include annual values and pricing basis
5. Management preferences include specific format requirements
6. The profile has been reviewed by the engagement partner

## Maintenance Schedule

- **Annual review**: Full review and update after each annual engagement
- **Interim updates**: When significant events occur (new contract, ownership change, regulatory action)
- **Pre-engagement check**: Before starting any new work, verify key facts are still current
